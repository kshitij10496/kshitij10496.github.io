Thought for the Day

I was thinking about a very ubiquitous topic that I like to explore more about - the topic of communication. Whether it be my curiousity about the origins of writing, or fascination with old/dead languages, I always like to think of language as a tool - a tool for communicating with others. It is true that attaining a certain level of proficiency at weilding this tool leads to a lot of opportunities. At the same time, I think that one’s command over a language is not actually a measure of one’s communication skills. You can clear GRE but still struggle to share your ideas with a group of people.

The point is - being effective at communication is an orthogonal skill to language proficiency.

Just being accurate with your grammar doesn’t make you John Green.

What brought me joy today?

  • Learning about the Evolution of the English alphabet
  • Learning 4 measures of Ode to Joy with C Major chords.
  • Finally setting up my BRITA water filter.
