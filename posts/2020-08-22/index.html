Thought of the Day

It’s like my very first memory, which I guess is the first time I was alive.
- Stephen Chbosky

Thinking about my first memory is actually much harder than it sounds, as if finding a needle in the haystack. Beside that I agree that a lot of our long term memories are moments that affect us to our core & events which shape something fundamental to our being. When I think about it, most of things I remember about my past life are such instances which either gave me great joy and some which made me realize the reality. Given the vast expanse of the universe, and my brief, transient existence I don’t know if it matters in the first place. To paraphrase from Murakami, the room where we store our memories is like our private library. And you’ll live forever in your own private library.

Maybe that is a worthy way to live - focusing on enriching your personal library with moments that do not make you feel infinitesimally small. ✨

What brought me joy today?

  • Observing a beautiful sunset sitting by the lake in the Citadelpark.
  • Light showers providing some respite from the summer heat.
